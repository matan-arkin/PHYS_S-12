Electronics and Tools

Simulation

I started with a straightforward LED circuit on tinkercad. To find the correct resistor value, I started by looking up the LED component in our kit. I went to MPJA's website and found the component. It claimed a 2v drop and 20mA of current. Here is the calculation I made using Ohm's Law to find the resistor value that should go in series with this LED:



To be safe for the real circuit, I chose a value of 100 ohms and built the simulation in Tinkercad:




Circuit

Next, I used a red LED from our kit, the Adafruit Metro Express M0 and a 100 ohm resistor to build the real circuit. It was really bright! Probably a high current...




I also measured the voltage drop on the LED. It turned out 2.07V, though I couldn't hold the camera and the prongs so you'll have to take that on faith :)




Microphone

I tried playing around with the microphone. I learned how to use the analogRead and analogReadResolution functions, trying 12-bit as well as 10-bit measurements. The log file and the code for 12-bit, adjusting for a 0.67V bias are available for download here. I was quiet for half the recording and then played a loud song for the other half. You can see the values jumping around in the second half of the log!
